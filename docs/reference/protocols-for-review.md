# Protocols Requiring Clinical Review

**Note:** The reference spreadsheet names have been renamed for clarity:
- "Med Chart 2021" has been renamed to **Spreadsheet 1**
- "Medication List 2021" has been renamed to **Spreadsheet 2**

The following protocols have discrepancies between the reference spreadsheets (Spreadsheet 1 and Spreadsheet 2), calculation concerns, or conflicts that require review by the clinical team before the protocol can be finalised.

---

## 1. Amoxicillin / Ampicillin — SPT Concentration

| Field | Spreadsheet 1 | Spreadsheet 2 |
|---|---|---|
| SPT | 1:5 (20 mg/mL) | Neat (100 mg/mL) |
| IDT | 1:10 + 1:5 | 1:100 |

**Issue:** The two spreadsheets disagree on both the SPT concentration and the IDT dilution series. Spreadsheet 1 specifies a 1:5 dilution for SPT (20 mg/mL) and IDT at 1:10 and 1:5, while the Medication List specifies neat (100 mg/mL) for SPT and IDT at 1:100 only.

**Current page status:** Updated to follow Spreadsheet 1 (authoritative source). Medication List alternative noted in page Notes.

**Action required:** Confirm which SPT concentration and IDT range is clinically correct.

---

## 2. Augmentin — Formulation and Concentrations

| Field | Spreadsheet 1 | Previous page |
|---|---|---|
| Stock vial | 500 mg powder | 1.2 g powder |
| SPT | 1:5 (2 mg/mL) | Neat (60 mg/mL) |
| IDT | 1:100 + 1:50 | 1:5 + 1:50 |

**Issue:** The page previously used a 1.2 g vial (1,000 mg amoxicillin + 200 mg clavulanate) reconstituted to 60 mg/mL. Spreadsheet 1 references a 500 mg formulation reconstituted to 10 mg/mL, with SPT at 1:5 (2 mg/mL) and IDT at 1:100 and 1:50.

**Current page status:** Updated to follow Spreadsheet 1 (500 mg formulation). The 1.2 g alternative noted in page Notes.

**Action required:** Confirm the correct stock formulation (500 mg vs 1.2 g) and verify the resulting test concentrations.

## 3. Tazocin — SPT Concentration Calculation

| Field | Page calculation | Spreadsheet 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Stock (reconstituted) | 200 mg/mL Piperacillin | — |
| SPT (1:10 of stock) | 20 mg/mL | 2 mg/mL |

**Issue:** The page calculates 1:10 of 200 mg/mL = 20 mg/mL Piperacillin. The Spreadsheet 2 spreadsheet specifies SPT at 1:10 (2 mg/mL). This is a 10× discrepancy that suggests either:
- The spreadsheet uses a different starting concentration, or
- There is an error in one of the values.

**Current page status:** Updated to reflect the spreadsheet value (2 mg/mL). The calculation discrepancy is flagged on the page.

**Action required:** Verify the correct SPT concentration. If 2 mg/mL is correct, the starting stock concentration or dilution factor needs clarification.

---

## 4. Pantoprazole — Spreadsheet Labelling Error

| Field | Page (correct) | Spreadsheet 2 |
|---|---|---|
| Stock vial | 40 mg powder | 40 mg powder |
| Reconstituted conc. | 4 mg/mL (40 mg + 10 mL NS) | Listed as "Neat (40 mg/mL)" |

**Issue:** The Spreadsheet 2 spreadsheet labels the SPT concentration as "Neat (40 mg/mL)". However, reconstituting a 40 mg powder vial with 10 mL NS yields **4 mg/mL**, not 40 mg/mL. The page calculation is correct; the spreadsheet has a labelling error.

**Current page status:** Page retained as-is (4 mg/mL is correct). Discrepancy noted in page Notes.

**Action required:** Correct the Spreadsheet 2 spreadsheet to read "Neat (4 mg/mL)" instead of "Neat (40 mg/mL)".

## 5. Ketamine — IDT Range

| Field | Spreadsheet 1 | Spreadsheet 2 | Spreadsheet note |
|---|---|---|---|
| IDT | 1:1,000 only | 1:100 | "ANZZAG: 1:1,000 Initial & Max, Protocol from department — 1:100, 1:10" |

**Issue:** Three different sources give three different answers:
- **Spreadsheet 1:** IDT at 1:1,000 only (most conservative)
- **Spreadsheet 2:** IDT at 1:100
- **Spreadsheet note:** ANZZAG specifies 1:1,000 as initial and max; department protocol uses 1:100 and 1:10

**Current page status:** Updated to follow Spreadsheet 1 (1:1,000 only). All alternatives noted in page Notes.

**Action required:** Confirm the correct IDT range. Determine whether the department protocol (1:100, 1:10) supersedes the Med Chart, and whether ANZZAG guidance should take precedence.

---

## 6. Ropivacaine — IDT Range

| Field | Spreadsheet 1 (GA Test) | Spreadsheet 2 |
|---|---|---|
| IDT | 1:10 only | 1:1,000, 1:100, 1:10, Neat |

**Issue:** Spreadsheet 1 specifies IDT at 1:10 only for the GA (general anaesthetic) test. The Spreadsheet 2 specifies a much fuller dilution series (1:1,000 → 1:100 → 1:10 → Neat).

**Current page status:** Updated to follow Spreadsheet 1 (1:10 only). Medication List alternative noted in page Notes.

**Action required:** Confirm whether the fuller IDT series is needed for certain clinical scenarios (e.g., local anaesthetic allergy testing vs GA testing).
