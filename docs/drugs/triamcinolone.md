---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Check the expiry date and vial integrity before opening. Label every syringe immediately after drawing up — never leave unlabelled syringes on the tray.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Triamcinolone 40 mg/mL vial
- Normal saline (NS) — for SPT dilution and IDT dilutions
- 1 mL syringes × 4
- 25 G needles

### Step 1 — Prepare the SPT solution (4 mg/mL — 1:10)
1. Draw up **1 mL** of stock (40 mg/mL) into a syringe. Add **9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **4 mg/mL — 1:10**).
2. Draw up **0.5–1 mL** of this diluted solution (4 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe.
3. Label: **"Triamcinolone SPT 4 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 0.04 mg/mL (1:1,000):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (40 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is 4 mg/mL — 1:10).
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of this 4 mg/mL into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.04 mg/mL — 1:1,000**).
    - Label: **"Triamcinolone IDT 0.04 mg/mL"**.

2. **IDT 0.4 mg/mL (1:100):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of the 4 mg/mL solution prepared above into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.4 mg/mL — 1:100**).
    - Label: **"Triamcinolone IDT 0.4 mg/mL"**.

3. **IDT 4 mg/mL (1:10):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (40 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **4 mg/mL — 1:10**).
    - Label: **"Triamcinolone IDT 4 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | 1:10 (4 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:1,000 | 0.04 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 4 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |
| 2 | 1:100 | 0.4 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 40 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |
| 3 | 1:10 | 4 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 40 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.
