# Amoxicillin

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Beta-lactam / aminopenicillin |
| Route | IV (for SPT/IDT); oral (for challenge) |
| Stock vial | 500 mg powder for injection |
| Reconstituted concentration | 100 mg/mL (add 5 mL WFI per Med Chart) |
| Diluent | Water for Injection (WFI) |
| Storage | Room temperature before reconstitution; 2–8°C after; use within 24 hours |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Check the expiry date and vial integrity before opening. Label every syringe immediately after drawing up — never leave unlabelled syringes on the tray.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Amoxicillin 500 mg powder vial
- 1 × 10 mL syringe (for reconstitution)
- Water for Injection (WFI) — 5 mL
- Normal saline (0.9% sodium chloride) — at least 15 mL (for dilutions)
- 1 mL syringes × 4 (one for SPT, three for IDT dilutions)
- 21 G needle (for vial entry)
- 25 G needles (for drawing up dilutions)
- Labels or marker pen

### Step 1 — Reconstitute the stock vial
1. Draw up **5 mL** of Water for Injection (WFI) into a 10 mL syringe.
2. Inject into the Amoxicillin 500 mg vial using aseptic technique.
3. Gently swirl the vial until the powder is completely dissolved. **Do not shake.**
4. The resulting stock solution is **Amoxicillin 100 mg/mL**. It should appear clear to slightly yellow. **Discard if cloudy, discoloured or if undissolved particles are visible.**
5. Write the date, time and your initials on the vial.

### Step 2 — Prepare the SPT solution (1:5 — 20 mg/mL)
1. Draw up **0.2 mL** of the 100 mg/mL stock into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Add **0.8 mL** of normal saline. Mix gently by inverting.
3. This gives **Amoxicillin 20 mg/mL** (SPT solution, 1:5 dilution). Label: **"Amoxicillin SPT — 20 mg/mL"**.

### Step 3 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 10 mg/mL (1:10):** Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (100 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. Label: **"Amoxicillin IDT 10 mg/mL"**.
2. **IDT 20 mg/mL (1:5):** Draw **0.2 mL** of stock (100 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.8 mL NS**. Mix. Label: **"Amoxicillin IDT 20 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | 1:5 (20 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:10 | 10 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of stock (100 mg/mL) + 0.9 mL NS |
| 2 | 1:5 | 20 mg/mL | 0.2 mL of stock (100 mg/mL) + 0.8 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.02–0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.

## Challenge (oral)

### Oral graded dose protocol
**Preparation:** Amoxicillin oral suspension 125 mg/5 mL (25 mg/mL)

| Step | Dose | Volume | Interval |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 12.5 mg | 0.5 mL | 20 min |
| 2 | 25 mg | 1 mL | 20 min |
| 3 | 50 mg | 2 mL | 20 min |
| 4 | 125 mg | 5 mL | 20 min |
| 5 | 250 mg | 10 mL | 60 min observation |

---

## Notes
- Observe a minimum of 30–60 minutes after the final dose.
- Have adrenaline available on standby before commencing.
- **Medication List alternative:** The Spreadsheet 2 spreadsheet lists SPT as Neat (100 mg/mL) and IDT as 1:100 only. This page follows Spreadsheet 1 which specifies SPT 1:5 (20 mg/mL) and IDT 1:10 + 1:5.
