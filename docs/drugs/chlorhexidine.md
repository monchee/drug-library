# Chlorhexidine

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Biguanide antiseptic |
| Stock vial | 0.02% solution (0.2 mg/mL) or 0.1% solution (1 mg/mL) |
| Reconstituted conc. | N/A (Ready to use) |
| Storage | Room temp; Protect from light |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Ensure the vial contains **Chlorhexidine 0.02% (0.2 mg/mL)**. Check expiry and integrity. Note that this is a common cause of perioperative anaphylaxis and must be tested if a patient reacts during surgery.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Chlorhexidine 0.2 mg/mL vial
- Normal saline (NS) — for IDT dilutions
- 1 mL syringes × 3 (for test doses)
- 25 G needles

### Step 1 — Prepare the SPT solution (0.2 mg/mL — neat)
1. Draw up **0.5–1 mL** of the stock (0.2 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Label: **"Chlorhexidine SPT 0.2 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 0.002 mg/mL (1:100):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (0.2 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is 0.02 mg/mL — 1:10).
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of this 0.02 mg/mL into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.002 mg/mL — 1:100**).
    - Label: **"Chlorhexidine IDT 0.002 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | Neat (0.2 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:100 | 0.002 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 0.02 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.
