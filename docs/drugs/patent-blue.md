# Patent Blue (Isosulfan Blue)

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Triphenylmethane dye / Lymphatic mapping agent |
| Stock formulation | 2.5% (25 mg/mL) or 10% (100 mg/mL) |
| Reconstituted conc. | N/A (Ready to use) |
| Storage | Room temp; Protect from light |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Check the concentration. Usually **2.5% (25 mg/mL)**. Note that Patent Blue and Isosulfan Blue are potent triggers for anaphylaxis and can interfere with pulse oximetry readings.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Patent Blue 25 mg/mL vial
- Normal saline (NS) — for IDT dilutions
- 1 mL syringes × 3 (for test doses/dilutions)
- 25 G needles

### Step 1 — Prepare the SPT solution (25 mg/mL — neat)
1. Draw up **0.5–1 mL** of the stock (25 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Label: **"Patent Blue SPT 25 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 0.025 mg/mL (1:1,000):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (25 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is 2.5 mg/mL — 1:10).
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of this 2.5 mg/mL into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is 0.25 mg/mL — 1:100).
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of this 0.25 mg/mL into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.025 mg/mL — 1:1,000**).
    - Label: **"Patent Blue IDT 0.025 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | Neat (25 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:1,000 | 0.025 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 0.25 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.
