# Clindamycin (Dalacin)

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Lincosamide antibiotic |
| Stock vial | 600 mg/4 mL (150 mg/mL) or 300 mg/2 mL (150 mg/mL) |
| Reconstituted conc. | N/A (Ready to use) |
| Storage | Room temp; Do not refrigerate |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Ensure the vial contains **150 mg/mL**. Check expiry and integrity. Note that this is a concentrated formulation.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Clindamycin 150 mg/mL vial
- Normal saline (NS) — for IDT dilutions
- 1 mL syringes × 2
- 25 G needles

### Step 1 — Prepare the SPT solution (150 mg/mL — neat)
1. Draw up **0.5–1 mL** of the stock (150 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Label: **"Clindamycin SPT 150 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 15 mg/mL (1:10):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (150 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **15 mg/mL — 1:10**).
    - Label: **"Clindamycin IDT 15 mg/mL"**.

---

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | Neat (150 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:10 | 15 mg/mL | 0.1 mL neat + 0.9 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.
**Delayed Reading:** If history is suggestive of a delayed reaction, re-read at 24 and 48 hours.
