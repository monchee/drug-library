# Methylprednisolone (Solu-Medrol)

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Corticosteroid / Glucocorticoid |
| Stock formulation | 1 g powder for injection |
| Reconstituted conc. | 20 mg/mL (add 5 mL NS) |
| Storage | Room temp before reconstitution; 2–8°C after |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Reconstitution"
    Methylprednisolone is typically reconstituted to **20 mg/mL** for skin testing. Ensure the 5 mL of Normal Saline (NS) is added and the solution is clear before use.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Methylprednisolone 1 g powder vial
- Normal saline (NS) — for reconstitution and dilutions
- 10 mL syringe
- 1 mL syringes × 4 (for test doses/dilutions)
- 25 G needles

### Step 1 — Reconstitute the stock (20 mg/mL)
1. Add **5 mL** of NS to the 1 g powder vial.
2. Shake gently until completely dissolved. (This is **200 mg/mL**).
3. Draw **0.1 mL** of this 200 mg/mL solution into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **20 mg/mL**).
4. Label: **"Methylprednisolone SPT 20 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 2 mg/mL (1:10):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (20 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **2 mg/mL — 1:10**).
    - Label: **"Methylprednisolone IDT 2 mg/mL"**.

2. **IDT 0.2 mg/mL (1:100):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of the 2 mg/mL syringe into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.2 mg/mL — 1:100**).
    - Label: **"Methylprednisolone IDT 0.2 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | Neat (20 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:100 | 0.2 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 2 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |
| 2 | 1:10 | 2 mg/mL | 0.1 mL neat + 0.9 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.
**Delayed Reading:** If history is suggestive of a delayed reaction, re-read at 24 and 48 hours.
