# Aspirin (ASA)

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | NSAID / Antiplatelet agent |
| Formulation | 300 mg tablets (Soluble) |
| Concentration | 10 mg/mL (Dissolve 300 mg in 30 mL water) |
| Storage | Room temp |

---

## Oral Graded Challenge (OGC)

!!! info "Preparation"
    Dissolve one **300 mg soluble Aspirin tablet** into **30 mL of water** to achieve a concentration of **10 mg/mL**. Use this solution for the initial steps or use fractional tablets.

### Protocol
| Step | Dose | Formulation | Cumulative Dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 30 mg | 3.0 mL of 10 mg/mL solution | 30 mg |
| 2 | 60 mg | 6.0 mL of 10 mg/mL solution | 90 mg |
| 3 | 150 mg | 1/2 Tablet | 240 mg |
| 4 | 300 mg | 1 Tablet | 540 mg |
| 5 | 600 mg | 2 Tablets | 1140 mg |

**Interval:** 60–90 minutes between steps as per clinical orders.

---

## Notes
- Aspirin-exacerbated respiratory disease (AERD) requires cautious challenge protocols.
- Monitor for asthma, rhinitis, or urticaria/angioedema during the challenge.
- If the patient is on a leukotriene antagonist (e.g., Montelukast), this should be noted as it may mask respiratory symptoms.
