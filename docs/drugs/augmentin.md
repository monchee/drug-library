# Augmentin (Amoxicillin / Clavulanate)

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Beta-lactam / aminopenicillin + beta-lactamase inhibitor |
| Route | IV (for SPT/IDT); oral suspension (for challenge) |
| Stock vial (IV) | 500 mg powder for injection (per Med Chart) |
| Reconstituted concentration | 10 mg/mL amoxicillin (add 50 mL WFI per Med Chart) |
| Oral suspension | Augmentin Duo 400/57 mg per 5 mL (80 mg/mL amox + 11.4 mg/mL clav) |
| Diluent | Water for Injection (WFI) |
| Storage | Use within 30 minutes of reconstitution at room temperature |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Check the expiry date and vial integrity before opening. Label every syringe immediately after drawing up — never leave unlabelled syringes on the tray.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Augmentin 500 mg powder vial
- 1 × 50 mL syringe (for reconstitution)
- Water for Injection (WFI) — 50 mL
- Normal saline (0.9% sodium chloride) — at least 10 mL (for dilutions)
- 1 mL syringes × 4 (one for SPT, three for IDT)
- 21 G needle (for vial entry)
- 25 G needles (for drawing up)
- Labels or marker pen

### Step 1 — Reconstitute the stock vial
1. Draw up **50 mL** of Water for Injection (WFI) into a 50 mL syringe.
2. Inject into the Augmentin 500 mg vial using aseptic technique.
3. Gently swirl until fully dissolved. **Do not shake.**
4. The resulting stock solution is **Amoxicillin 10 mg/mL**. It should appear clear to pale yellow. **Discard if cloudy or if particles are visible.**
5. Write the date, time and your initials on the vial.

### Step 2 — Prepare the SPT solution (1:5 — 2 mg/mL)
1. Draw up **0.2 mL** of the 10 mg/mL stock into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Add **0.8 mL** of normal saline. Mix gently by inverting.
3. This gives **Amoxicillin 2 mg/mL** (SPT solution, 1:5 dilution). Label: **"Augmentin SPT — 2 mg/mL"**.

### Step 3 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 1 mg/mL (1:10):** Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (10 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. Label: **"Augmentin IDT 1 mg/mL"**.
2. **IDT 0.1 mg/mL (1:100):** Draw **0.1 mL** of the 1 mg/mL syringe into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. Label: **"Augmentin IDT 0.1 mg/mL"**.
3. **IDT 0.2 mg/mL (1:50):** Draw **0.2 mL** of the 1 mg/mL syringe into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.8 mL NS**. Mix. Label: **"Augmentin IDT 0.2 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | 1:5 (2 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:100 | 0.1 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 1 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |
| 2 | 1:50 | 0.2 mg/mL | 0.2 mL of 1 mg/mL + 0.8 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.

## Challenge (oral)

### Oral graded dose protocol
**Preparation:** Augmentin Duo 400/57 suspension — shake well before each dose.

| Step | Dose | Volume | Interval |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 25/3.6 mg | 0.3 mL | 30 min |
| 2 | 100/14.3 mg | 1.25 mL | 30 min |
| 3 | 275/39.4 mg | 3.44 mL | 30 min |
| 4 | 875/125 mg | 10.9 mL | 60 min observation |

## Notes
- Augmentin contains amoxicillin plus clavulanate — both components should be noted in documentation.
- Observe a minimum of 30–60 minutes after the final dose.
- Have adrenaline drawn up and labelled before commencing.
- **Alternative formulation:** A 1.2 g vial (1,000 mg amoxicillin + 200 mg clavulanate, reconstituted with 20 mL WFI to 60 mg/mL amoxicillin) may be available. This page follows Spreadsheet 1 which specifies the 500 mg formulation.
