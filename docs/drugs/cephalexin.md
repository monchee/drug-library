# Cephalexin

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | 1st generation cephalosporin |
| Route | Oral (challenge); IV (SPT/IDT) |
| Forms | 250 mg, 500 mg tablets; 125 mg/5 mL, 250 mg/5 mL suspension |
| Stock (SPT/IDT) | 2 mg/mL solution prepared by Pharmacy |
| Storage | Room temp before; refrigerate suspension after mixing |

---

## Preparation for Skin Testing

!!! info "Before you start"
    The SPT/IDT stock solution (2 mg/mL) is prepared by Pharmacy. Confirm availability before the test appointment. Label every syringe immediately after drawing up.

### Equipment needed
- Cephalexin 2 mg/mL stock solution (prepared by Pharmacy)
- Normal saline (NS) — for IDT dilutions
- 1 mL syringes × 5 (one for SPT, four for IDT dilutions)
- 25 G needles
- Labels or marker pen

### Step 1 — Prepare the SPT solution (2 mg/mL — neat)
1. Draw up **0.5–1 mL** of the Pharmacy-prepared stock (2 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Label: **"Cephalexin SPT 2 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 0.2 mg/mL (1:10):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (2 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.2 mg/mL — 1:10**).
    - Label: **"Cephalexin IDT 0.2 mg/mL"**.

2. **IDT 0.02 mg/mL (1:100):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of the 0.2 mg/mL syringe into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.02 mg/mL — 1:100**).
    - Label: **"Cephalexin IDT 0.02 mg/mL"**.

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | Neat (2 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:100 | 0.02 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 0.2 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |
| 2 | 1:10 | 0.2 mg/mL | 0.1 mL neat + 0.9 mL NS |
| 3 | Neat | 2 mg/mL | Undiluted stock |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.

## Preparation for Oral Challenge

!!! info "Before you start"
    Ensure the patient has fasted if required. Use a calibrated oral syringe for all volumes. Check the concentration of the suspension (125 mg/5 mL or 250 mg/5 mL).

### Reagents
- Cephalexin Suspension (e.g., Keflex)
- Syringes: 1 mL, 3 mL, 5 mL, 10 mL oral syringes

### Step-by-step Challenge Protocol

#### Using 125 mg/5 mL (25 mg/mL) Suspension:
| Step | Dose | Volume | Cumulative Dose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 25 mg | 1.0 mL | 25 mg |
| 2 | 50 mg | 2.0 mL | 75 mg |
| 3 | 100 mg | 4.0 mL | 175 mg |
| 4 | 250 mg | 10.0 mL | 425 mg |

---

## Challenge Protocol
**Intervals:** Observe for **20 minutes** between each step.
**Final Observation:** Monitor for **1–2 hours** after the final dose.

---

## Notes
- Cephalexin is the most common oral 1st generation cephalosporin.
- Cross-reactivity with [Amoxicillin](amoxicillin.md) and [Ampicillin](ampicillin.md) is common due to shared side chains.
- SPT/IDT stock solution (2 mg/mL) must be prepared by Pharmacy in advance.
