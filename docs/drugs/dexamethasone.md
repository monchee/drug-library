# Dexamethasone

## Overview
| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| Drug class | Corticosteroid / Glucocorticoid |
| Stock formulation | 4 mg/mL or 8 mg/2 mL (4 mg/mL) |
| Reconstituted conc. | N/A (Ready to use) |
| Storage | Room temp; Protect from light |

---

## Preparation

!!! info "Before you start"
    Ensure the vial contains **4 mg/mL**. Check expiry and integrity.

### Equipment needed
- 1 × Dexamethasone 4 mg/mL vial
- Normal saline (NS) — for IDT dilutions
- 1 mL syringes × 2
- 25 G needles

### Step 1 — Prepare the SPT solution (4 mg/mL — neat)
1. Draw up **0.5–1 mL** of the stock (4 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe.
2. Label: **"Dexamethasone SPT 4 mg/mL"**.

### Step 2 — Prepare IDT dilutions
Prepare in this order, using a fresh syringe for each step.

1. **IDT 0.04 mg/mL (1:100):**
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of stock (4 mg/mL) into a 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is 0.4 mg/mL — 1:10).
    - Draw **0.1 mL** of this 0.4 mg/mL into a new 1 mL syringe. Add **0.9 mL NS**. Mix. (This is **0.04 mg/mL — 1:100**).
    - Label: **"Dexamethasone IDT 0.04 mg/mL"**.

---

## Skin prick test (SPT)
| Reagent | Concentration |
|---|---|
| Test solution | Neat (4 mg/mL) |
| Positive control | Histamine 10 mg/mL |
| Negative control | Normal saline |

**Interpretation:** Wheal ≥3 mm vs negative control = positive.

---

## Intradermal test (IDT)

### Dilution series
| Step | Dilution | Concentration | Preparation |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 1:100 | 0.04 mg/mL | 0.1 mL of 0.4 mg/mL + 0.9 mL NS |

**Inject:** 0.05 mL intradermal. Read at 15–20 minutes.
**Delayed Reading:** If history is suggestive of a delayed reaction, re-read at 24 and 48 hours.
